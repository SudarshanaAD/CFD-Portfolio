Results Summary – Backward-Facing Step (Laminar Flow)

Residuals:
- Residuals converged to ~1e-6 after ~500 iterations.
- Indicates numerical stability of the solution.

Velocity Field:
- Pathlines colored by velocity magnitude show high velocity at the inlet (red).
- Flow expands after the step, velocity decreases downstream (green → blue).
- Recirculation bubble visible near the bottom wall downstream of the step.

Pressure Field:
- Pressure contours show a drop across the step.
- Low-pressure region formed inside the recirculation bubble.

Wall Shear Stress:
- X-wall shear stress along the downstream wall remains negative (from ~–1.7e–21 Pa to ~–1.2e–05 Pa).
- Indicates sustained reverse flow along the wall.
- No zero-crossing observed → reattachment point lies outside the simulated domain.

Interpretation:
- Simulation correctly captures flow separation at the step.
- Recirculation bubble extends beyond the outlet length (3 m downstream).
- Current geometry insufficient to capture reattachment length within domain.

Limitations:
- Outlet too close → reattachment not observed.
- Mesh refinement near wall may need improvement.
- Future runs should extend downstream length (≥15–20 × step height) to capture reattachment point.

Next Steps:
- Redesign geometry with longer downstream channel.
- Perform grid independence study.
- Compare reattachment length with literature values once captured.
